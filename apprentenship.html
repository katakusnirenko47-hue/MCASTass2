What is an Apprenticeship?

An apprenticeship at MCAST allows students to combine classroom learning with hands-on work experience. Students gain industry skills while earning a qualification.

Apprenticeships are available across multiple sectors and help students prepare for real employment.

Learning Skills

During an internship, individuals develop both technical and soft skills. Technical skills are the hands-on abilities required in a specific field, like coding, graphic design, laboratory techniques, or financial analysis. Soft skills, on the other hand, include communication, teamwork, time management, and problem-solving. Interns learn how to collaborate with colleagues, meet deadlines, and adapt to professional expectations."

Understanding the Workplace

Internships provide a unique insight into the daily operations of a company or organization. Interns learn about workplace culture, organizational structure, and professional etiquette. This experience helps them understand how their role contributes to the larger goals of the company and prepares them for future responsibilities.

Networking and Career Exploratio

An internship is also an opportunity to build professional relationships. Interns meet mentors, supervisors, and peers who can offer guidance, references, and advice. They can explore different career paths, discovering what areas they are passionate about and what skills they want to develop further.
